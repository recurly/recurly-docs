    <!-- Section: How multi-currency works -->
    <div class="rc-lp-section">
      <h2><i class="fa-solid fa-globe rc-fa-section"></i> How multi-currency works in Recurly</h2>
      <p>When multi-currency is enabled, you define a separate price for each currency on each plan. Recurly presents the subscriber's currency to your payment gateway and processes the charge in that currency — no conversion happens inside Recurly itself.</p>
      <p>This matters because your prices are independent per currency. If you charge $10 USD and £10 GBP, those are two distinct price points you control — not an exchange-rate calculation. You decide the local price; Recurly and your gateway handle the rest.</p>

      <div class="rc-card-grid">
        <div class="rc-feature-card">
          <div class="rc-feature-icon"><i class="fa-solid fa-money-bill-transfer"></i></div>
          <h4>No Recurly conversion</h4>
          <p>Recurly does not convert currencies. Each currency's price is set independently on the plan. Your gateway processes the charge in the subscriber's currency and settles to your merchant account.</p>
        </div>
        <div class="rc-feature-card">
          <div class="rc-feature-icon"><i class="fa-solid fa-lock"></i></div>
          <h4>Currency is set at subscription creation</h4>
          <p>When a subscription is created, the currency is locked for the life of that subscription. It cannot be changed after creation — plan this before going live in a new market.</p>
        </div>
        <div class="rc-feature-card">
          <div class="rc-feature-icon"><i class="fa-solid fa-sliders"></i></div>
          <h4>Independent pricing per currency</h4>
          <p>Each currency gets its own price on the plan. You can price strategically for each market rather than relying on exchange-rate equivalents.</p>
        </div>
        <div class="rc-feature-card">
          <div class="rc-feature-icon"><i class="fa-solid fa-credit-card"></i></div>
          <h4>Gateway compatibility required</h4>
          <p>Your payment gateway must support each currency you enable. Recurly shows you which currencies your current gateway configuration can handle before you activate them.</p>
        </div>
      </div>

    <div class="rc-lp-section">
      <h2><i class="fa-solid fa-circle-plus rc-fa-section"></i> How to enable a new currency</h2>
      <p>Currency setup is a two-step process: enable the currency on your site, then add prices for that currency to your plans.</p>

      <div class="rc-steps">
        <div class="rc-step">
          <div class="rc-step-num">1</div>
          <div class="rc-step-content">
            <h4>Check gateway compatibility</h4>
            <p>Go to <strong>Configuration → Currencies</strong>. The Currencies Configuration page shows which currencies your current gateway setup can process. Confirm your target currency is listed before enabling it.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="rc-step">
          <div class="rc-step-num">2</div>
          <div class="rc-step-content">
            <h4>Enable the currency</h4>
            <p>From the Currencies Configuration page, add the currency to your accepted list. Multi-currency support must be active on your account — contact Recurly Support if it isn't enabled yet.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="rc-step">
          <div class="rc-step-num">3</div>
          <div class="rc-step-content">
            <h4>Add prices to your plans</h4>
            <p>Go to <strong>Configuration → Plans</strong> and edit each plan you want to offer in the new currency. Add a price for the new currency in the plan's pricing section. Existing subscriptions are not affected.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="rc-step">
          <div class="rc-step-num">4</div>
          <div class="rc-step-content">
            <h4>Update coupons if applicable</h4>
            <p>If you use coupons, add pricing for the new currency to any active coupons that should apply to subscribers in that market. Coupons without a price defined for a currency cannot be redeemed in that currency.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="rc-callout rc-callout-caution">
        <div class="rc-callout-icon"><i class="fa-solid fa-triangle-exclamation" style="color:#FF8200;"></i></div>
        <div class="rc-callout-body">
          <strong>Recurly does not perform currency conversion</strong>
          <p>The amount you set for a currency is the exact amount charged — there is no automatic conversion from your base currency. If you enable GBP but don't set a GBP price on a plan, subscribers in that currency cannot subscribe to it. Set prices deliberately for every currency you enable.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>

    <!-- Section: Pricing strategy -->
    <div class="rc-lp-section">
      <h2><i class="fa-solid fa-chart-bar rc-fa-section"></i> Pricing strategy across currencies</h2>
      <p>Because each currency's price is independent, you have two approaches. Neither is wrong — your choice depends on your market strategy.</p>

      <div class="rc-table-wrap">
        <table class="rc-table">
          <thead>
            <tr>
              <th>Approach</th>
              <th>How it works</th>
              <th>Best for</th>
            </tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td>Exchange-rate parity</td>
              <td>Set prices that roughly mirror your base currency converted at current rates. Simpler to maintain; may require periodic adjustments as rates shift.</td>
              <td>Businesses entering a new market quickly, or where local pricing perception isn't a key factor.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td>Local market pricing</td>
              <td>Set prices based on what the local market will bear — not exchange-rate equivalents. GBP 8 instead of the USD 10 equivalent of GBP 7.90, for example.</td>
              <td>Businesses prioritizing conversion in specific markets, or where local price anchors differ significantly from the base currency.</td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>

      <div class="rc-callout rc-callout-tip">
        <div class="rc-callout-icon"><i class="fa-solid fa-lightbulb" style="color:#0D0D0B;"></i></div>
        <div class="rc-callout-body">
          <strong>Round local prices intentionally</strong>
          <p>Subscribers notice odd prices. If your USD price is $9.99, set your GBP price to £7.99 or £8.99 — not the exact exchange-rate equivalent. Clean price points convert better and signal a localized experience rather than a converted one.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>

    <!-- Section: What to decide before enabling -->
    <div class="rc-lp-section">
      <h2><i class="fa-solid fa-circle-question rc-fa-section"></i> What to decide before enabling a new currency</h2>

      <div class="rc-accent-card rc-accent-yellow">
        <h4>Four questions to answer upfront</h4>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Does your payment gateway support this currency?</strong> Check the Currencies Configuration page before enabling. Adding a currency your gateway can't process creates a broken checkout path.</li>
          <li><strong>What price will you set — parity or local market?</strong> Decide your pricing strategy before setting prices on plans. Changing prices later affects only new subscribers; existing ones keep their original price.</li>
          <li><strong>Will you offer all plans in this currency, or a subset?</strong> You can enable a currency site-wide but only add prices to select plans. Subscribers in that currency can only sign up to plans with a price defined for their currency.</li>
          <li><strong>Do your active coupons need to support this currency?</strong> Check all active coupons and add the new currency price before launching. A coupon without a price for a currency cannot be applied to subscriptions in that currency.</li>
        </ul>
