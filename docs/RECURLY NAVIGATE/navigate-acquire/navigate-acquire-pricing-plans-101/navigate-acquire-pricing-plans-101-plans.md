    <!-- Section: What is a plan -->
    <div class="rc-lp-section">
      <h2><i class="fa-solid fa-layer-group rc-fa-section"></i> What is a plan?</h2>
      <p>A plan is the template for a subscription. Every subscriber signs up to a plan — it defines what they get, how much they pay, and when they're billed. You configure plans once; Recurly applies those settings to every subscription created from that plan automatically.</p>

      <div class="rc-card-grid">
        <div class="rc-feature-card">
          <div class="rc-feature-icon"><i class="fa-solid fa-calendar-days"></i></div>
          <h4>Billing interval</h4>
          <p>How often the subscriber is charged — weekly, monthly, quarterly, annually, or a custom interval. Cannot be changed after the plan is created.</p>
        </div>
        <div class="rc-feature-card">
          <div class="rc-feature-icon"><i class="fa-solid fa-clock-rotate-left"></i></div>
          <h4>Subscription term</h4>
          <p>The commitment length. A term can span multiple billing periods (e.g., an annual plan billed monthly). Adjustable per subscription at creation.</p>
        </div>
        <div class="rc-feature-card">
          <div class="rc-feature-icon"><i class="fa-solid fa-arrow-rotate-right"></i></div>
          <h4>End-of-term behavior</h4>
          <p>Whether subscriptions auto-renew or expire at the end of each term. Set at the plan level; override per subscription if needed.</p>
        </div>
        <div class="rc-feature-card">
          <div class="rc-feature-icon"><i class="fa-solid fa-tag"></i></div>
          <h4>Pricing model</h4>
          <p>Fixed, ramp, or usage-based. Determines how the amount charged is calculated each billing period. Covered in detail on the Pricing models page.</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="rc-callout rc-callout-caution">
        <div class="rc-callout-icon"><i class="fa-solid fa-triangle-exclamation" style="color:#FF8200;"></i></div>
        <div class="rc-callout-body">
          <strong>Billing interval is permanent</strong>
          <p>Once a plan is created, you cannot change its billing interval — doing so would disrupt active subscriptions. If you need a different interval, create a new plan. Plan your billing intervals before you start building.</p>

      <p>Most subscription businesses offer both billing intervals. The data supports making annual plans a priority: subscribers on annual plans generate 50–60% more revenue per year and churn significantly less. Based on 2026 industry benchmarks, 78% of subscription businesses offer both monthly and annual options.</p>

      <h2><i class="fa-solid fa-circle-plus rc-fa-section"></i> How to create a plan</h2>

        <div class="rc-step">

          <div class="rc-step-content">
            <h4>Navigate to Plans</h4>

          <div class="rc-step-num">2</div>
          <div class="rc-step-content">
            <h4>Set your plan code</h4>
            <p>Enter a plan code — this is your internal identifier used in API calls. Use something descriptive and consistent (e.g., <strong>monthly-basic</strong>, <strong>annual-pro</strong>). It cannot be changed after saving.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="rc-step">
          <div class="rc-step-num">3</div>
          <div class="rc-step-content">
            <h4>Configure billing settings</h4>
            <p>Set your billing interval, subscription term length, and end-of-term behavior. These determine the subscription's lifecycle. Billing interval cannot be changed after creation.</p>

          <div class="rc-step-num">4</div>
          <div class="rc-step-content">
            <h4>Set price and pricing model</h4>

          <div class="rc-step-num">6</div>
          <div class="rc-step-content">

    <div class="rc-lp-section">
      <h2><i class="fa-solid fa-circle-question rc-fa-section"></i> Things to decide before you build</h2>
      <p>A few decisions are hard to reverse once subscribers are on a plan. Settle these before creating your first plan.</p>

      <div class="rc-accent-card rc-accent-yellow">
        <h4>Questions worth answering upfront</h4>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>How many billing intervals do you need?</strong> Start with monthly and annual. Adding more later is easy; migrating subscribers to a different interval is not.</li>
          <li><strong>What is your plan code convention?</strong> Establish a consistent naming pattern now — it affects API integrations and reporting downstream.</li>
          <li><strong>Will you offer both monthly and annual from launch?</strong> If yes, create both plans before going live. Annual discounts drive LTV and should be part of your initial checkout experience.</li>
          <li><strong>Does your pricing model match your product's value metric?</strong> If you charge per seat, per usage, or per outcome — fixed pricing may not fit. Review the Pricing models page before committing.</li>
